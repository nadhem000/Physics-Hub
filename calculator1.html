1. Definitions and Basic Concepts

Molar Mass (M)

The mass of one mole of a substance, expressed in grams per mole (g/mol). It is numerically equal to the atomic or molecular mass in atomic mass units (u).

M = m/n

Mass (m)

The actual mass of a sample, typically measured in grams (g).

Amount of Substance (n)

The number of moles of a substance, representing 6.022 × 10²³ particles (atoms, molecules, or formula units).

Avogadro's Number

6.022 × 10²³ particles per mole - the number of atoms in exactly 12 grams of carbon-12.

NA = 6.022 × 10²³ mol⁻¹

2. Rules for Writing Chemical Formulas

Element Symbols

Use standard chemical symbols: H for hydrogen, O for oxygen, C for carbon, etc.

Subscripts

Numbers after element symbols indicate atom count: H₂O has 2 hydrogen and 1 oxygen atom.

Parentheses

Groups in parentheses are multiplied by the following subscript: Ca(OH)₂ has 1 Ca, 2 O, and 2 H atoms.

Hydrates

Use a dot for hydrates: CuSO₄·5H₂O indicates copper sulfate pentahydrate.

Case Sensitivity

Element symbols are case-sensitive: Co is cobalt, CO is carbon monoxide.

Charges

For ions, include charges: SO₄²⁻ for sulfate ion.

Formula Examples:

H₂O NaCl C₆H₁₂O₆ Ca(OH)₂ Al₂(SO₄)₃ CuSO₄·5H₂O Fe₂O₃ CH₃COOH

3. Calculation Methods

Calculating Molar Mass from Mass and Amount

M = m ÷ n

Where M is molar mass (g/mol), m is mass (g), and n is amount of substance (mol).

Example

If you have 18.0 g of water and it represents 1.0 mole, the molar mass is:

M = 18.0 g ÷ 1.0 mol = 18.0 g/mol

Calculating Mass from Molar Mass and Amount

m = M × n

Where m is mass (g), M is molar mass (g/mol), and n is amount of substance (mol).

Example

If the molar mass of NaCl is 58.44 g/mol and you have 0.5 moles, the mass is:

m = 58.44 g/mol × 0.5 mol = 29.22 g

Calculating Amount from Mass and Molar Mass

n = m ÷ M

Where n is amount of substance (mol), m is mass (g), and M is molar mass (g/mol).

4. Practice Exercises

Exercise 1: Simple Compound

Calculate the molar mass of carbon dioxide (CO₂).

Carbon: 12.01 g/mol × 1 = 12.01 g/mol
Oxygen: 16.00 g/mol × 2 = 32.00 g/mol
Total: 12.01 + 32.00 = 44.01 g/mol

Exercise 2: Compound with Parentheses

Calculate the molar mass of calcium nitrate, Ca(NO₃)₂.

Calcium: 40.08 g/mol × 1 = 40.08 g/mol
Nitrogen: 14.01 g/mol × 2 = 28.02 g/mol
Oxygen: 16.00 g/mol × 6 = 96.00 g/mol
Total: 40.08 + 28.02 + 96.00 = 164.10 g/mol

Exercise 3: Hydrate Compound

Calculate the molar mass of copper(II) sulfate pentahydrate, CuSO₄·5H₂O.

Copper: 63.55 g/mol × 1 = 63.55 g/mol
Sulfur: 32.06 g/mol × 1 = 32.06 g/mol
Oxygen: 16.00 g/mol × 4 = 64.00 g/mol
Water: 5 × (2×1.008 + 16.00) = 5 × 18.016 = 90.08 g/mol
Total: 63.55 + 32.06 + 64.00 + 90.08 = 249.69 g/mol

Exercise 4: Mass Calculation

What is the mass of 2.5 moles of sodium chloride (NaCl)?

Molar mass of NaCl = 22.99 + 35.45 = 58.44 g/mol
Mass = 2.5 mol × 58.44 g/mol = 146.10 g

Exercise 5: Moles Calculation

How many moles are in 50.0 g of glucose (C₆H₁₂O₆)?

Molar mass of C₆H₁₂O₆ = (6×12.01) + (12×1.008) + (6×16.00) = 180.16 g/mol
Moles = 50.0 g ÷ 180.16 g/mol = 0.278 mol
